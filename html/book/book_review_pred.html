Story of KING

   Reviews, as the direct message from customers, are the KING of Amazon. But, what does the KING look like? In this part of analysis, we try to answer this question.

   KING looks differently. With sentiment intensive analyser, we “easily and directly predict” the score from how positive the review is. The result is surprising but reasonable that 5-star reviews are in general positive while the sentiments of 1-star reviewers vary greatly. Frustrated readers are not only complainers but may be suggesters. Secondly, we measure how the reviews are structured by counting the usage of different type of word as well as question mark and exclamation point. Again, the results are intuitive. Question mark is preferred by disappointed readers; exclamation point undoubtably is the first choice of 5-star reviewers. One-star reviewers like adverbs which includes nearly all negatives.

   KING looks similarly. Looking deeply into the reviews, we find the most frequently used words in reviews of all 5 rates. And, a large part of those words of each rating are in common. No matter what the score is, reviewers focus on the same topics and use similar words—adjectives and nouns. When we introduce machine learning to “predict” our KING, he seems not very happy. We fail to group reviews of 5-rate into 2 groups with high prediction accuracy, which, we “boldly” guess, results from the diversity of personal measuring standard. For example, just as we discuss above, 1-star reviewers are much nicer and more “positive” than we image.

   There’re many KINGs. Out of 30 categories, we pick 6 most unlikely overlapping reviews sets and build the word-clouds. “LOVE” is the key word for romance and “FAIRY TALES” are always the best friends of children; you cannot become successful in business without “INFORMATION” and you cannot become successful in “LIFE” without rich experience and memory; “DOG” nowadays has been the member of family and “HEALTH” is everyone’s concern. Furthermore, if 30 KINGs are standing in front of you in a row, you can definitely point out who they are because they do not look alike each other.

   Normally, the analysis should end up with something more conclusive but in this time we refuse to do so. Because that’s what the KINGs are, that’ what the READERs are, and that’s what WE are.
