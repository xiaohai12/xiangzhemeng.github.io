Price plays an important role?   It’s tricky to answer.

   First ,let’s have a look at price with average rating. We plot the average rating of each price group as well as the number of books in each group based on our dataset. There is some trend that higher price results in higher score, which is more or less a doubtable conclusion as we have fewer data in high price groups. But, one thing is for sure, low price does not lead to high average ratings, and perhaps people are more care about the value of books comparing to the price.

   Now, turn our attention to reviews. Followings are the average length of reviews of each price group with the number of reviews. Lower price leads to shorter pages which results in shorter length of reviews. And if the price is too high for short reads, the reviewers may not willing to write that long review again. But, after all, there are always personal effects behind it. We cannot draw any easy conclusion here.


Conclusion

   Will short reads be continuously popular? Our answer is yes. As the pace of life keep speeding up, there seems little time left for us young people to concentrate on reading. And that’s when short reads stands up. However, as we found out, short reads is not that popular and people love to read those longer reads. Because of varying quality of short reads and inattentive readers, ‘true-short’ reads cannot make themselves the hits and attract more people to write reviews promoting them. The short reads are needed in our life and we look forward to having some really fantastic writers who create the short but superb reads for us.
