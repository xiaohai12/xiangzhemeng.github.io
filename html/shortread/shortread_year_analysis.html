Kindle Short Read

   “Great Stories in One Sitting” is how Amazon promotes its recently announced section of Kindle Books — Short Reads, which only contains stories from 1 to 100 pages in length and with reading time from 15 minutes to 120 minutes. Arising from 2014, Short Reads now takes up almost one quarter of all available English Kindle titles on Kindle market.

   There have been a lot of discussions on the topics of short reads, the book page lengths as well as the price. Let’s have a look at some recent opinions and facts. Here we want to show you whether the common belief is supported by the recent fact base collected from Amazon.

Nowadays, reader loves short reads?   Well, not really.

   Reader satisfaction, as expressed by book review ratings increases with the length of the book; conversely, average ratings drop the shorter the book — at least in the Short Reads segment. So does the sales of books, which are roughly approximated by the number of reviews collected in the dataset.

   From the ratings breakdown, the difference among the length of books becomes more intuitive and informative. As what we have learnt from the book store, most of the reviewers are all very kind to give 4 or 5 star and only less than a quarter of them score the book three star or even lower. However, despite of this general truth, 15-minutes short reads are also not that welcomed with only less than a half of ratings of 5 star.


   But, why? One of possible reason is that, writing a short reads is never a piece of cake. How to attract readers in a 15-minute reading or even less is the acid test of a good author. After all, not everybody is as talented as O.Henry. On the other hand, short reads are never prepared for intensive reading but just as the name suggesting for short and quick readings, maybe waiting at train station or on your way home. So, what can you expect for the readers after only quick skimming?
