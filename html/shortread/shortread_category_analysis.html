Short reads lead to short review?   Sounds correct.

   The first time we drew the box-plot below, we thought we just made some rubbish. However, after rethinking, actually the outliners give us very valuable information—Amazon has very good review tradition and good review atmosphere. As the length of the book increases, there are going to be more and more active and “serious” reviewers who wrote extreme long reviews. People love to write longer review after finishing longer readings, which of course makes senses.

   Although the outliners provide us some useful insight about the Amazon review atmosphere, we have to remove them to see the major distribution. Following are the mean and median of length of reviews corresponding to each category.

   Longer read does lead to longer review. However, the mean length is much longer than median length and the difference of the median is not that clear as that of mean. These two facts indicate that most of reviews written by the majority of reviews have similar length, and the longer the book is, the more the “serious” reviews there are.
